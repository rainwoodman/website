I am a Cosmology Data Science Fellow at Berkeley Center for Cosmological Physics. I am also affliated with Berkeley Institute for Data Science.

My work spans three fields: Cosmology, extreme-scale super-computing applications, and tools for scientific data science.

Here is a list of research topics and projects that I am interested in and/or have contributed to:

  • Cosmology
    • Large Scale Structure and cosmology; [5] [7] [16]
    • Formation of galaxies, galaxy clusters, star-formation models and super-massive blackholes. [2] [1] [3] [4]
    • Weak lensing and intrinsic alignments. [6]
    • Reduction of imaging data from imaging surveys. [18]
  • Super-computing Applications
    • Solver for cosmological hydrodynamics; [8]
    • Parallel Fast Fourier Transformation; [9]
    • Massively parallel sorting; [10]
    • Python on super-computers; [11]
  • Scientific Data Analysis
    • Parallel data analysis of N-body simulations [12]
    • Hierarchical Data Format for large data sets [17]
    • KD-tree and pair-wise counting [13] [14]
    • Visualization of smoothed particle field [15]
